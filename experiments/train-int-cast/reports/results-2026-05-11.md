# Training int-cast: first run results
2026-05-11. See ../plan.md and ../EXPERIMENT_LOG.md.

## TL;DR

Pure logit-L2 distillation onto fp32 weight shadows of every IntLinear
(STE backward, norms/RoPE/embeddings frozen) **does not close the
int-cast → bf16-reference gap** under the budgets we tried. At lr=1e-6
the student diverges within hundreds of steps; at lr=1e-7 (Luke's
default) the student barely moves over 2500 steps — every headline
metric stays within its initial ±10% noise band, with no monotone
improvement on any.

This matches Luke's own observation that the bulk of the residual
error after int conversion lives outside the matmuls.

## Setup
- **Models**: Qwen2.5-0.5B (this report). Qwen3-8B and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct
  in progress, see below.
- **Conversion**: full int-cast (weights + activations, 16 effective bits),
  same as the baseline experiment. Trainable IntLinear path uses fp32
  weight_fp shadows with straight-through quant in forward and identity
  gradient routing.
- **Loss**: mean over all valid positions (no masking padding) of
  `||logits_ref(x) - logits_int(x)||² / V`.
- **Optimizer**: AdamW (8-bit for 8B via bitsandbytes), wd=0, cosine
  decay from peak lr to 10% over 5000 steps, 500-step linear warmup,
  grad-norm clip 1.0.
- **Data**: 10k wikitext-103-raw-v1 prompts × 512 tokens, batch=4 prompts.
  Held-out 100-prompt eval set used for every metric report.
- **Stopping**: plateau early-stop on all-positions top-1 (3 successive
  evals with no improvement, after step 500 + 3 × eval cadence).

## Qwen2.5-0.5B at lr=1e-6 (aborted)

The original plan started at lr=1e-6. Killed at step 500 once trajectory
was clear:

| step | top-1 | logit-L2 mean | margin mean | margin p99 |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 0 | 0.9790 | 21.08 | 1.09e-03 | 4.10e-02 |
| 250 | 0.9736 | 37.21 | 2.25e-03 | 7.92e-02 |
| 500 | 0.9712 | 36.63 | 2.92e-03 | 9.35e-02 |

Training loss was *decreasing* (0.023 → 0.009) but all eval metrics
were getting worse — the student was overfitting per-batch logit-L2
in a way that didn't generalize. lr=1e-6 is too aggressive for a model
already 97.9% top-1 aligned with the teacher; AdamW's normalized step
amplifies a small gradient into ~lr per parameter per step, and the
direction it picks doesn't preserve held-out behavior.

## Qwen3-8B and Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct (in progress)

In progress on 2 additional vast boxes. Status to be filled in once
the runs complete. Initial pre-train evals match baseline exactly
(0.9828 / 0.9882), so the trainable-mode setup is numerically clean.

8B runs hit two ops issues during the first attempts:
1. **80 GB VRAM not enough without grad checkpointing** — the fp32
   gradient buffer for 7.5B params is 30 GB on top of 30 GB shadows +
   14 GB 8-bit Adam state + 16 GB teacher. Even on a 140 GB H200 the
   activation memory on top pushed total over 137 GB. Added
   `--grad-checkpoint` flag wiring `model.gradient_checkpointing_enable`
   on the student; recomputes activations in backward (~+30% step time)
   in exchange for ~40 GB freed.
2. **80 GB disk not enough for repeated 30 GB checkpoints** — each
   intermediate save was 30 GB (7.5B params × 4 bytes fp32). Several
   crashed-save partials filled disk. Switched to bf16 checkpoints
   (~15 GB) with atomic replace and per-run rotation (delete old when
   saving new). Skipped the step-0 save entirely.

## Interpretation

Three plausible reasons pure logit-L2 doesn't move the needle:

1. **The residual is outside the matmuls.** Luke's claim from earlier
   work: matmul divergence is ~0, the gap comes from RMSNorm / RoPE /
   softmax which we leave float. Training Linear weights can only
   compensate for matmul-attributable error; if the error doesn't live
   there, the optimizer has no purchase on the metric.
2. **lr=1e-7 with 5000 steps is far below Luke's training scale.**
   Luke ran ~50k+ steps. Our 2500 is two orders of magnitude short.
   "Linear-only doesn't help" might just mean "Linear-only doesn't help
   on this budget" — could move with 100× more compute.
3. **Pure logit-L2 has a weak signal at this regime.** The mean over
   vocab is dominated by near-zero tail contributions; the gradient
   that actually moves the head token gap is a vanishingly small
   fraction of the total. Luke pairs logit-L2 with a per-matmul
   normalized MSE auxiliary that gives the optimizer a localized
   signal at every Linear. We dropped it for the first cut.

## Suggested next moves (in priority order)
1. **Add the per-matmul aux loss** (Luke's other ingredient). Cheap
   intervention; ships the next-most-informative gradient signal to
   the layers we expect to do most of the work.
2. **Quantize + train the norms.** If hypothesis 1 is right (norms
   own the residual), this is the actual fix.
3. **Cooler 5e-7 LR with a 50k-step budget** before declaring lr=1e-7
   dead. Cost: ~2 h on 0.5B. Risk: same result, +$8.
4. **KL-distillation with temperature** instead of L2. Re-weights the
   loss toward head tokens.
