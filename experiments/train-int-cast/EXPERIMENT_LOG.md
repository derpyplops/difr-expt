# Experiment log: train-int-cast
Started 2026-05-11. See experiments/train-int-cast/plan.md.

- 2026-05-11: Plan drafted. Starting model is Qwen2.5-0.5B for cheap iteration and direct parity with Luke's `int-model-approximation`. Objective: pure logit-L2 between bf16 teacher and int-cast student, all positions; STE on float-shadow weights, norms/RoPE/embeddings frozen. Budget: 5k steps × batch 2048 tokens, LR sweep {1e-7, 1e-6, 1e-5}, ~2 h on H100. Pass criteria: all-positions top-1 ≥ 99.9% (currently 0.9788 on 0.5B). Differences from Luke recorded in plan.md: simpler loss (no per-matmul aux to start), more data (10k prompts), broader LR sweep, dual-aggregation eval. Per-matmul aux is the explicit fallback if logit-L2 alone plateaus.
- 2026-05-11: Implementation — extended `IntLinear` with optional `weight_fp: nn.Parameter` (float shadow) + STE forward path (`fake_quantize_per_row_ste`, `fake_quantize_per_token_ste`). Added `freeze_to_int` baking helper so the final checkpoint runs through the unchanged inference path. Wrote `cache_prompts.py` (one-shot wikitext tokenize → `prompts.pt`), `train.py` (teacher-forced logit-L2, AdamW with optional bnb 8-bit for 8B, cosine warmup, eval every 250 steps, plateau early-stop). 8 new tests in `tests/test_train_step.py` cover STE gradient flow, weight_fp update, end-to-end loss decrease on toy, freeze-to-int roundtrip. All 26 tests across baseline + train pass.
- 2026-05-11: Provisioning — running 3 boxes in parallel: existing H100 SXM (36511132, $3.80/h) for Qwen2.5-0.5B, new H100 SXM (36515464, $3.55/h) for Qwen3-8B, new H200 (36515465, $4.18/h) for Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct. Same `pytorch/pytorch:2.5.1-cuda12.4-cudnn9-devel` image, rsync deploy. 8B boxes additionally install `bitsandbytes 0.49.2` for 8-bit AdamW (fp32 shadows + fp32 m,v would bust 80 GB at 8B scale; bnb cuts optimizer state to ~14 GB and brings 8B comfortably under 75 GB).
- 2026-05-11: First-run divergence at lr=1e-6. Kicked all 3 off at lr=1e-6, batch=4 prompts × 512 tokens, warmup=100, 5k steps. On Qwen2.5-0.5B, eval top-1 went 0.9790 → 0.9736 → 0.9712 over the first two eval cycles (step 250/500). Training loss simultaneously dropped (0.023 → 0.009). Diagnosis: the student is overfitting to per-batch logit-L2 while drifting away from teacher behavior on held-out prompts. lr=1e-6 from initialization that's already 0.979 top-1 puts too much push on a tiny gradient signal, and AdamW's normalization amplifies it. Luke's lr=1e-7 default looks correct after all; the plan's "1e-6 baseline, sweep up to 1e-5" was wrong. Killed all 3 at step ~500 (0.5B), step ~0 (8B; the 8B run wasn't far enough in to show the same pattern but assuming it would, we restarted preemptively). Relaunched all 3 at **lr=1e-7, warmup=500** (5× longer warmup gives more time to detect divergence before going full lr). Added `python -u` + `sys.stdout.reconfigure(line_buffering=True)` to fix nohup buffering — training step prints were 4KB-chunked and made progress invisible.
- 2026-05-11: Qwen2.5-0.5B at lr=1e-7 plateau-stopped at step 2500 (442 s wallclock). Every headline metric stayed inside its starting noise band: top-1 in [0.9774, 0.9806] (best +0.16pp above start, well within 1pp 95% CI for n=100 eval), logit-L2 21.08 → 21.28 (slightly *worse*), margin mean 1.09e-3 → 1.16e-3 (slightly worse), KL 1.22e-3 → 1.23e-3 (flat). Training loss bounced 3-5e-3 with no downtrend. Net: pure logit-L2 on weight_fp shadows of every Linear, norms frozen, **did not move the model in any direction the verifier cares about** at this LR / step budget. Plausible reasons in priority order: (a) the residual lives in the un-quantized norms/RoPE per Luke's hypothesis, so Linear-shadow gradients have no purchase on the metric; (b) lr=1e-7 × 2500 steps is 100× short of Luke's training scale (he ran ~50k+); (c) the vocab-mean L2 has its signal dominated by tail tokens, not the head-gap that determines top-1. Reported in reports/results-2026-05-11.md.
- 2026-05-11: 8B ops issues. First 8B relaunch (v2) OOM'd at ~80 GB on H100 (Qwen3-8B) and ~137 GB on H200 (Llama-8B): a fp32 gradient buffer for 7.5B params is 30 GB on top of 30 GB shadows + 14 GB bnb 8-bit Adam state + 16 GB bf16 teacher, leaving little headroom for activations. Added a `--grad-checkpoint` flag wired to `model.gradient_checkpointing_enable(gradient_checkpointing_kwargs={"use_reentrant": False})` on the student. Second relaunch (v3) crashed because `grad_checkpoint` wasn't added to the `Config` dataclass — fixed. Third relaunch (v4) crashed during `save_ckpt(0)` because the 30 GB fp32 step-0 checkpoints filled the 80 GB vast disk after a few crash-restarts (`runs/` directory accumulated 65 GB of partial saves). Switched checkpoints to bf16 (~15 GB), skipped step-0 save entirely, added atomic-rename + delete-previous so disk holds at most one ckpt at a time. Cleaned each box's `runs/` and relaunched again. Sequence of operational fixes (grad-checkpoint, Config field, disk discipline) cost a real ~30 min of clock time but each one had to surface before the next could.
- 2026-05-11: Qwen3-8B abandoned. v4 launch with grad checkpoint OOM'd again at 77 GB on the 80 GB H100 (instance 36515464) — even with the activations checkpointed away, the bnb 8-bit Adam state initialization on first step bumps memory by ~14 GB and there's no room. Math: 16 (teacher) + 30 (fp32 shadows) + 30 (fp32 grad) + 14 (bnb state) = 90 GB, which doesn't fit on 80 GB no matter how aggressive the checkpointing. Real fixes are (a) bf16 shadows + bf16 grad — costs ~half the STE precision; (b) CPU-offloaded paged AdamW — costs ~20% step time; (c) bigger box (H200 / B200). Killed the box (no clean data point recovered, paid ~$15) to stop bleeding $3.55/hr while iterating. Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct on the H200 (140 GB, instance 36515465) survived with 108 GB peak and is training normally — that's the 8B data point we'll report. Lesson: 8B QAT with fp32 shadows wants ≥140 GB VRAM; 80 GB requires either lower-precision shadows or CPU offload.
- 2026-05-11: Llama-8B at lr=1e-7 trending wrong direction. Through step 500 (end of warmup): top-1 0.9882 → 0.9859 (-0.23pp), logit-L2 11.93 → 15.06 (+26%), margin mean 6.89e-4 → 8.37e-4 (+21%), margin p99 1.98e-2 → 2.93e-2 (+48%). Same "pure logit-L2 mildly destructive" pattern we saw on the 0.5B at lr=1e-6, just slower. The 8B's larger parameter count means each AdamW-normalized step has a larger downstream effect, so 1e-7 on Llama-8B may be acting like 1e-6 did on 0.5B. Letting it ride to the plateau-stop for a clean comparison rather than killing early.
- 2026-05-11: Aux-loss + long-budget experiment kicked off. Added `--aux-weight` flag + forward-hook plumbing on every Linear (teacher + IntLinear) for Luke-style per-matmul normalized-MSE auxiliary loss. Loss is now `logit_l2 + aux_weight × mean_over_Linears(||s-t||²/||t||²)`. Local smoke test on a toy net confirms hooks fire on the right modules and produce sensible NMSE values. Launched on Qwen2.5-0.5B (box 36511132) with: 50000 steps (20× the previous plateau), aux_weight=1.0, lr=1e-7, warmup=500, eval-every=1000, plateau_patience=6 (so the early-stop doesn't fire prematurely at this longer scale). Estimated ~2.75 h wallclock. If this also plateaus near 0.9790, it's strong evidence that pure-Linear QAT genuinely can't close this gap on this model regardless of step budget, and the residual must live in the un-quantized norms/RoPE/softmax. If it actually moves, we have a recipe.
